Words That Inspire

A collection of powerful quotes from some of the greatest minds and voices in history. Read their words, feel their wisdom, and let them inspire you to think bigger and dream further.

Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist — (1879–1955)

Albert Einstein once said that imagination is more important than knowledge, a reminder that creative thinking is the true engine of discovery.

"The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing..."

— Albert Einstein

Malala Yousafzai

Education Activist & Nobel Peace Prize Laureate — (Born 1997)

Malala powerfully declared that one child, one teacher, one book, and one pen can change the world, showing us that even the smallest acts of learning carry enormous power.

"I raise up my voice — not so that I can shout, but so that those without a voice can be heard..."

— Malala Yousafzai

Nelson Mandela

Anti-Apartheid Leader & Former President of South Africa — (1918–2013)

Nelson Mandela believed that education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world, a truth he lived by throughout his entire life and struggle.

"It always seems impossible until it is done. Do not judge me by my successes; judge me by how many times I fell down and got back up again..."

— Nelson Mandela

Marie Curie

Physicist & Chemist, First Woman to Win a Nobel Prize — (1867–1934)

Marie Curie reminded the world that nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood, encouraging us to face the unknown with curiosity rather than fear.

"Be less curious about people and more curious about ideas. Life is not easy for any of us. But what of that? We must have perseverance and confidence in ourselves."

— Marie Curie
